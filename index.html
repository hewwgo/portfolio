PhD Fellow at Aarhus University · hugo@cs.au.dk · LinkedIn

I'm a first-year PhD Fellow in Human-Centered Computing at Aarhus University, advised by Niklas Elmqvist. My background is in cognitive science, and I now work across HCI, AI, and design research.

So far my PhD has taken me through creativity tools, collective knowledge systems, and agentic simulations — different entry points into the same question: what changes when AI becomes part of how people think, create, and collaborate?

Papers

  1. Tessera: Faceted Exploration of Collective Knowledge for Collaborative Ideation Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist · UIST 2026 (under review) arXiv ↗
    Abstract Captures a research group's collective knowledge as a navigable map of tiles, then generates ideation directions constrained by the group's actual submissions.
  2. TombWriter: Scaffolding Story Archeology through Beat-Level Interaction in Human-AI Co-Writing Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist · AVI 2026 PDF ↗ arXiv ↗
    Abstract Treats LLM prompts as a hierarchical story instrument refined over time. Writers work at the level of story beats rather than prose, generating character actions to excavate their story.
  3. Material for Thought: AI as Creative Medium, Not Tool Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist · CHI 2026, Tools for Thought workshop arXiv ↗
    Abstract Position paper arguing that AI is better understood as a creative medium — something you shape and push against — than a tool that executes instructions.
  4. From Role to Person: Trust Calibration Challenges in Twin Agents Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist · CHI 2026, AutomationXP workshop arXiv ↗
    Abstract Examines trust calibration challenges when agents move from playing roles to simulating specific people in human-in-the-loop automation.
