Hugo Andersson

PhD Fellow·Human-Centered Computing·Aarhus University

I'm a first-year PhD Fellow in Human-Centered Computing at Aarhus University, advised by Niklas Elmqvist. Background in cognitive science. Now working across HCI, AI, and design research.

So far my PhD has covered creativity tools, collective knowledge systems, and agentic simulations — different entry points into the same question: what changes when AI becomes part of how people think, create, and collaborate?

Papers

  1. UIST 2026 · Under review Tessera — Faceted Exploration of Collective Knowledge for Collaborative Ideation Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist

    What if a research group could browse its own collective thinking? Tessera turns scattered notes and half-formed ideas into a visual map, and helps surface questions nobody could find alone.

  2. AVI 2026 · Venice TombWriter — Scaffolding Story Archeology through Beat-Level Interaction in Human-AI Co-Writing Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist
    arXiv PDF

    Most AI writing assistants forget the moment you walk away. TombWriter remembers — and lets you sculpt a story one beat at a time, not one sentence. Closer to directing actors than typing prose.

Workshops

  1. CHI 2026 · Tools for Thought workshop Material for Thought — AI as Creative Medium, Not Tool Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist
    arXiv PDF

    AI usually gets pitched as a tool that does what you ask. This argues for the opposite: treat it as a material you wrestle with, like clay. What changes about how we build tools for thinking then?

  2. CHI 2026 · AutomationXP workshop From Role to Person — Trust Calibration Challenges in Twin Agents Hugo Andersson, Niklas Elmqvist
    arXiv PDF

    When you ask an AI agent to act like a specific person, how do you know it's actually getting them right? A look at the strange trust gaps that open when agents stop playing roles and start simulating people.
